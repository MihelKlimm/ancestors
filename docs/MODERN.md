# The last 2,000 years, and the last 100 — sources and caveats

## Paternal I-M423 / I-CTS10228 ("Dinaric") — dating and the Slavic question

**Dating (SNP/aDNA, which supersede the older inflated STR ages):**
- I-CTS10228 radiation node (I-Y3120) TMRCA ≈ **2,100–2,200 years** — Iron Age.
  Fóthi et al. 2020: "the CTS10228 group is 2200 ± 300 years old." Milewski &
  Dawidziuk 2025: I-Y3120 ≈ 2,442 ybp (95% CI 2825–2110).
- **Essentially absent from ancient remains until the Early Middle Ages.** Earliest
  L621 anywhere is Copper-Age Hungary (~4000 BCE, sample I8797); CTS10228 not in any
  sample until ~650 CE.

**The Slavic-expansion link — demonstrated migration, contested ethnic label:**
- The migration itself is well evidenced: Olalde 2023 (*Cell*) and Gretzinger 2025
  (*Nature*, 555 genomes) show >80% gene-pool turnover in parts of Central Europe and
  the Balkans in the 6th–8th c., with strong patrilocality, sourced from north of
  Ukraine / south of Belarus.
- **But "CTS10228 = Slavic ethnic marker" is an inference, not settled.** The lineage's
  founder predates Slavic ethnogenesis by centuries (Fóthi's own caveat: "cannot solely
  be tied to the Slavs"). Kushniarevich 2015 reads the South-Slav I2a peak as a
  *pre-Slavic Balkan substrate* assimilated by incoming Slavs. Gretzinger 2025
  foregrounds R1a-M458, not CTS10228, and is explicitly agnostic on the ethnolinguistic
  identity of any single lineage.
- Fringe/nationalist framings (Eupedia/Anthrogenica forums, the "Dacian/Burebista"
  hypothesis) are kept out of the page — aDNA shows Y3120 only post-700 CE.

**Note on conflation, verified against primary data:** three separate I2 branches are
routinely mixed up. Only the M423→L621→CTS10228 line is the "Dinaric/Slavic" one.
Loschbour and Motala sit on the M423 backbone but *off* L621. The Iron Gates, Dnieper
and Baltic Mesolithic I2 belong to the *separate* M223 branch.

## Modern frequencies (approximate; vary between studies)

Paternal I2a-Dinaric: Herzegovina ~65–71%, Bosnian Croats ~64–73%, Dalmatia ~50–65%,
Serbia ~37–42%, Belarus/W. Ukraine ~18–22%, Poland ~9–10%. Modern Croatia ≈39% I2a
overall. Herzegovina holds the world peak.

Maternal U5/U5b: Sami ~40–48% (much of it U5b1b1 — the northern peak), Finns ~20%,
Basques ~13%, Baltic ~10%. U5 as a whole is thin and pan-European.

## Material culture (§5 drawings)

The costume/architecture/object illustrations are **general ethnographic depictions**,
not documentation of any specific family. Dinaric detail (Morava-house/kula construction,
opanci, šajkača, jelek, gusle) and Sami detail (gákti, lavvu, noaidi drum) draw on
standard ethnographic literature; specific construction figures came partly from a
research agent's training knowledge rather than a live citation, and are presented as
description, not cited fact.
