under a free licence. Attribution below is required by those licences and is also
shown on the page itself.

## Deliberately NOT used

**The 2018 Cheddar Man facial reconstruction** (the widely-reproduced bust with
dark skin and blue eyes) is **not usable**, despite appearing on Wikipedia.

The only Commons copy is a CC BY-SA 2.0 *photograph* of a **2018 sculpture by
Alfons and Adrie Kennis**, commissioned by Channel 4 and the Natural History
Museum. The sculpture itself remains in copyright, and the file page carries no
freedom-of-panorama tag for the underlying three-dimensional work. Commons
tolerates it under a UK CDPA s.62 argument (sculptures permanently sited in
premises open to the public) that is **defensible but untested**, and which would
not hold in most other jurisdictions.

Every other version of that bust — NHM website, press photography, Channel 4
stills — is straightforwardly copyrighted.

So the page uses the skull instead, and any reconstructed face shown is **our own
drawing**, labelled as such.

**La Braña 1** has *no* freely licensed image of any kind — verified three ways
against the Commons API, including a zero-result search across all namespaces.
The familiar blue-eyed reconstruction is a commissioned CSIC-associated work and
is not free. That individual is therefore represented by our own illustration.

## Fetching notes

Wikimedia rejects on-demand thumbnail generation at arbitrary widths for scripted
clients — 320/400/640/800 px returned HTTP 400/429, while **500 px and 960 px**
returned 200. Use those widths, or resize locally after downloading. Requests
should send a descriptive `User-Agent`.
