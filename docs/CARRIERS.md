# Documented carriers — evidence and grading

Who *documentably carried* the same haplogroups. A shared haplogroup makes
someone a relative on that single line — a shared ancestor, often millennia back
— **not** an ancestor. This file grades every claim so the page can separate
what is established from what is folklore.

**Grades:** **A** = peer-reviewed study on remains, or a documented pedigree to a
living tester. **B** = reputable genetic-genealogy project, one inferential step
short. **C** = unsourced, commercial, or forum claim — recorded here only to be
refuted.

## The headline

- **No Grade-A cultural figure** (scientist, artist, composer, writer,
  philosopher, architect, engineer) carries I-M423 or U5b. Wikipedia's *List of
  haplogroups of historic people* has **zero** U5 entries and one I2a1b entry
  (Martin Luther — Grade C, see below).
- **No reigning dynasty** is documented on either line.
- The strongest positive is **regional nobility, not royalty**: two named Bosnian
  *knezovi*.

## Grade A — named carriers

| Person | Date / place | Call | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Loschbour man** | 6220–5990 BC, Luxembourg | Y **I2a-M423\***, mt **U5b1a** | Own remains sequenced (Lazaridis 2014, *Nature*). Carries **both** target lines in one person. M423 refinement is from SI + reanalysis. |
| **Knez Mirko Radojević** | late 14th c., Kopošići, Bosnia | Y I2a-Din *(B)* | Named magnate under Tvrtko I; buried under a gilded silver mantle. Father–son link to Batić proven autosomally + Y-STR (Džehverović 2021, *Int. J. Osteoarch.*, **A**). |
| **Knez Batić Mirković** | early 15th c., Kopošići | Y I2a-Din *(B)* | *Knez* under Tvrtko II. Same necropolis: **6 of 7 males I2a-Din**, one R1a. |

> The Bosnian haplogroup is **Y-STR-predicted, not SNP-tested**, and the paywalled
> PDFs were not opened — so it is not yet confirmed the two *named* knezovi are in
> the I2a majority rather than the lone R1a. This is the closest thing to
> "documented nobility + documented I2a-Din" that exists, and confirming those two
> PDFs is the single highest-value follow-up.

## Grade A — archaeological (anonymous sample IDs unless named)

**U5b:** Cheddar Man (~7150 BC, U5b1, also Y-I2a); La Braña 1 (~5000 BC, U5b2c1);
Villabruna (~12000 BC, U5b2b); "Neve" infant, Arma Veirana (~10000 BP, U5b2b);
Young Man of Byrsa, Carthage (6th c. BC, U5b2c1); Djehutynakht (~2000 BC, U5b2b5
— *authors flagged contamination*).

**I-M423 / I-CTS10228:** Hungarian Conqueror elite, Karos (10th c., L621 — the
best-documented ancient I2a-Din cluster); Sungir 6 (medieval, ~1100–1220 CE,
inside CTS10228 — *not* the Palaeolithic Sunghir 1–4, who are C1a2); Viking-age
VK542 Chernihiv / VK53 Gotland / VK210 Kraków (Margaryan 2020). Early-medieval
Slavic-migration burials show I2a-L621 appearing **only after ~700 CE**, always
with Eastern-European autosomal ancestry — introduced *by* the Slavic migration,
absent from Balkan antiquity.

## Grade A — famous lineages that are NOT on these branches

Romanovs (mt T2/H) · Richard III (mt J1c2c3, Y G) · Árpáds (R1a-Z2123) · Piasts
(R1b) · Rurikids (N1a) · Bourbons (R1b-U106) · Beethoven (**I1**, not I2) ·
Copernicus (mt H) · Jefferson (T) · Petrović-Njegoš of Montenegro (E-V13).

## Grade C — actively discard

- **Tesla = I2a** — false; a documented Tesla descendant tested R1a; he was
  cremated, no remains exist.
- **Martin Luther = I2a-Din** — surname-matching + iGENEA marketing (a known
  fabrication source); no verified descendant.
- **Henri IV of France = U5b** — from a mummified head now rejected as a
  misidentification; no Y-match to living Bourbons.
- **VK542 = "Prince Gleb" (Rurikid)** — the sample is genuinely I-Y3120, but the
  *name* is not in the source; Zhur 2023 says it cannot be substantiated.
- **Corrections:** Motala is U5a/U2e, *not* U5b. Bad Dürrenberg shaman is U4b1b1.
  Ötzi is K1f.

## Why the absence is structural

Documented haplogroups need exhumed-and-studied remains or an unbroken pedigree to
a living tester. Both conditions have been met overwhelmingly for Western European
royalty and canonical composers — exactly where I-M423 and U5b are *rarest*. The
Balkans, where I-CTS10228 peaks (30 %+), has had almost no elite-remains genomics.
And I-CTS10228 is so common (~10–35 % across Slavs) that any single noble
descendant testing I2a-Din is statistically unremarkable without a documented
pedigree. The absence is a fact about who gets studied, not about your ancestry.

## Method note

The researching agent disclosed that it had, mid-task, written up invented interim
"findings" and then retracted them; every claim retained here was independently
sourced afterward. Recorded because the brief was explicitly about guarding
against fabricated haplogroup claims — the standard applies to the tooling too.
